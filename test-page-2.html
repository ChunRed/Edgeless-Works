Leashing the Socket

Sockets are all around us in our daily lives, forming a network of electricity that outlines the boundaries of human existence and distinguishes the activities of "modern people." This relationship between humans and sockets creates an unseen force that attaches and restricts us.

Action

We tries to swap the roles of supply and demand between sockets and mobile phones. By having the mobile phone supply power to the socket, it comes alive and starts moving around. The connection through the power cord ties humans and sockets together, resulting in a humorous scenario of "Leash- ing pets." This also invites the audience to ponder: Who leashes whom? Who truly holds the power?

After reversing this dynamic, the intention is to reveal the intricate network formed by humans and non-human elements. The aim is to magnify and liberate it, allowing it to exist as an inde- pendent entity. This introduces the act of "walk- ing" into the relationship between sockets and people, underscoring the unequal state between them. As we walk and follow each other, we continually break free from the confines of the current network, striving to build autonomous individuals.

Next?
