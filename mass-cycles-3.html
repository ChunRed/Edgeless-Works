數位循環｜Mass Cycles 

< 2024 新北市美展 - 金獎 >

現實環境正不斷轉換為資訊，使人們試圖去尋找能證明「資訊」存在的方法，當我們所認為虛擬的事物確實存在著，環境的變遷、水文的循環將因數位化而改變，數據統計、數位化保存等亦改變了人們認知的自然，我們所認為的真實漸漸從虛擬中得知，使現實蒸發為虛擬又凝結為真實。然而數位與虛擬納為自然的循環中，我們喪失了何物？在一次次數位的循環後又將遺忘哪些本質的意義？數位循環｜

The transformation of our physical environment into information makes people seek ways to validate "information's" existence. As what we perceive as virtual elements indeed exists, shift natural cycles through digitization, altering our grasp of the natural world. Reality unfolds from the virtual, evaporating into the digital and solidifying back into reality. Amidst this digital-virtual cycle, what's lost? Which essential meanings fade after repeated digital shifts?

「數位循環」嘗試將虛擬地景介入現實環境當中，藉此捕捉數位循環中所喪失的「資訊的重量」。透過實地掃描自然景觀後，運用點雲技術複製出虛擬地景，計算其資訊位元的重量並與現實地景想比對，最後將消逝的重量透過霧化裝置噴出等重量的水霧消散至空間中，正如那些數位化後所蒸發至無的現實樣貌，龐大且充斥在周遭，正在消逝卻無人察覺。

"Mess Cycle" introduces virtual landscapes to physical environments, capturing lost "information weight." Scanning natural scenes and recreating virtual ones using point cloud tech, we calculate their informational weight and compare with real surroundings. The vanishing weight disperses through misting, paralleling the evaporation of digitized realities. Imperceptible yet vast, these shifts engulf us, fading away undetected.
